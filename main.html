Understanding Waste Types

🥬

Biodegradable Waste

What is it? Organic materials that break down naturally through microbial action.

Examples: Food scraps, vegetable peels, fruit waste, leaves, grass clippings, paper towels, tea bags, coffee grounds.

Why segregate? Can be composted to create nutrient-rich soil for plants, reducing landfill waste by 30-40%.

♻️

Recyclable Waste

What is it? Materials that can be processed and converted into new products.

Examples: Plastic bottles, glass jars, aluminum cans, cardboard, newspapers, magazines, metal items.

Why segregate? Saves energy, reduces pollution, conserves natural resources, and decreases greenhouse gas emissions.

☠️

Hazardous Waste

What is it? Toxic materials that can harm human health and the environment.

Examples: Batteries, paint, pesticides, cleaning chemicals, expired medications, fluorescent bulbs.

Why segregate? Prevents soil and water contamination, protects wildlife, and ensures safe disposal through specialized facilities.

💻

Electronic Waste

What is it? Discarded electronic devices and appliances.

Examples: Smartphones, laptops, tablets, TVs, refrigerators, washing machines, printers, cables.

Why segregate? Contains valuable materials (gold, silver) and toxic substances (lead, mercury) that require specialized recycling.

💡 Waste Management Tips

🏠 At Home

Keep separate bins for different waste types. Rinse recyclables before disposing. Compost kitchen waste in your backyard or use community composting facilities.

🛒 While Shopping

Bring reusable bags and containers. Choose products with minimal packaging. Buy in bulk to reduce waste. Opt for recyclable or biodegradable packaging.

♻️ Reduce & Reuse

Repair items instead of replacing them. Donate or sell items you no longer need. Use reusable water bottles, coffee cups, and food containers.

🌱 Composting

Start a compost bin for food scraps and yard waste. Avoid composting meat, dairy, or oily foods. Turn compost regularly for faster decomposition.

📱 E-Waste Management

Take old electronics to certified e-waste recycling centers. Delete personal data before disposing devices. Consider donating working devices to charity.

☠️ Hazardous Waste

Never pour chemicals down drains. Store hazardous materials in original containers. Take to designated collection centers, not regular trash.
